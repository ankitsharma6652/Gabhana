## History — Fort Gabhana / Gabhana Palace
- Gabhana estate of the Jadaun (Jadon) Rajputs, descendants migrated from Karauli State
- One of the oldest Jadon Rajput settlements of the Aligarh–Hathras region; the Riyasat once comprised ~60–80 villages, revenue ~₹70,000/yr in the 1930s
- Oldest palace sections ~250–300 years old (some sources: 1825–1835); built at the site of an earlier mud fort
- Newer wing built by Laxmi Raj Singh and Capt. Dev Raj Singh, ~1912–1917/1919 (5 years to complete)
- Architecture: Rajasthani pink-sandstone carved gateway, double chhatris, jali work, sheesh mahal, hand-painted domed ceilings, red-oxide verandahs, katchehri (court chamber); pale yellow-and-white colonial wings
- Stone carvings found during construction suggest an ancient temple once stood at the site
- One family branch (Dipendra Raj Singh; daughter Stuti) has been restoring it as a heritage homestay, with Rajasthani artisans repairing painted ceilings
